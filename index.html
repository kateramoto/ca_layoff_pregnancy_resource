Start Here

The moment you're laid off, do this.

Being let go while pregnant is overwhelming. These immediate steps protect your benefits and keep your options open.

1

Get everything in writing

Request your layoff notice, final paystub, and any severance details by email immediately. Once a company is gone or HR goes quiet, this paperwork is nearly impossible to recover — and EDD needs it.

2

File for Unemployment right away

If you're currently able and available to work (even light duties), file for UI immediately. You don't have to collect it for long — but filing protects you from income gaps before disability begins.

3

Talk to your doctor now

Your physician's certification is the key to every benefit below. Have a frank conversation about when they'll certify you as unable to perform your regular job duties. Timing this correctly is critical.

4

Create your myEDD account

Register at myedd.edd.ca.gov before you need to file. Having your account ready means faster processing when you transition between benefits — delays often happen during account setup under stress.

5

Understand your base period

Your benefits are based on wages earned 5–18 months before your claim start date — not your current employment status. Layoff does NOT eliminate your eligibility. Your contributions still count.

6

Check your COBRA deadlines

Health insurance ends with employment (or at end of severance). You typically have 60 days to elect COBRA. Medi-Cal is also an option if income drops — apply through Covered California.

Your Entitlements

Three benefits you may qualify for

California has some of the most robust protections for pregnant workers in the country. Being laid off does not mean you lose access to these programs — your eligibility is based on your earnings history, not your current job status.

Phase 1

Unemployment Insurance (UI)

Up to$450/week (varies by earnings)

UI provides income replacement while you're unemployed and able to work. After a layoff, this is typically your first bridge while you're still available for work. Once pregnancy prevents you from working, you'll transition to SDI — but UI can cover the gap in between.

Duration
Up to 26 weeks
Benefit Amount
60–70% of recent wages
When to file
Immediately after layoff
Key requirement
Must be able & available to work
  • Must have lost your job through no fault of your own (layoff qualifies)
  • Must be actively looking for work and able to accept suitable employment
  • You'll certify every two weeks — answer questions about your availability honestly
  • You CANNOT collect UI and SDI/PFL at the same time — notify EDD when you transition
  • You CAN return to UI after PFL ends if you're still job searching

Phase 2

State Disability Insurance (SDI)

Up to$1,765/week (2026 maximum)

SDI covers you when pregnancy prevents you from performing your regular job duties. This is YOUR money — deducted from your paycheck as CASDI. Being laid off doesn't eliminate this benefit. Eligibility is based on your earnings during the base period (roughly 5–18 months before your claim), not your current employment.

Benefit Amount
70–90% of base period wages
Typical Duration
4 wks pre-birth + 6–8 wks post
Minimum Earnings
$300 in base period
Claim Form
DE 2501 (online or mail)
  • Eligibility based on earnings 5–18 months before claim start — layoff status doesn't matter
  • Standard coverage: 4 weeks before due date, 6 weeks after vaginal delivery, 8 weeks after C-section
  • Your doctor must certify you are unable to perform your regular job duties — being pregnant alone is not enough
  • File no earlier than 9 days after your disability begins; file within 49 days to avoid losing benefits
  • Do NOT file too early — EDD can deny claims where doctor hasn't certified inability to work
  • If complications arise, your doctor can certify earlier or extend your claim beyond standard duration
  • Workers earning under ~$62,000/year may receive up to 90% wage replacement

Phase 3

Paid Family Leave (PFL) — Baby Bonding

Up to$1,765/week (2026 maximum)

After your SDI pregnancy disability ends, you transition to PFL to bond with your newborn. This is a separate benefit — you must file a new claim. PFL is available to both parents regardless of employment status at birth, as long as you have qualifying base-period wages.

Duration
Up to 8 weeks
Benefit Amount
60–90% of base period wages
Claim Form
DE 2501FP (New Mother)
When to file
When SDI disability ends
  • File when your doctor certifies recovery from pregnancy disability — this is your signal to switch
  • EDD will send you a DE 2501FP link in your SDI Online inbox — watch for it and act quickly
  • If the automatic link doesn't appear, call EDD proactively — this is a known system glitch
  • PFL must be used within one year of the child's birth
  • You can return to UI after PFL ends if you are available for work and job searching
  • Both parents can claim PFL baby bonding — partners can file separately

Critical Timing

Your benefits roadmap

The order and timing of these claims matters enormously. Here's the sequence that protects you from gaps and avoids overpayment penalties.

Unemployment Insurance

Day 1 after layoff → File for UI immediately

Apply right away at edd.ca.gov. You're unemployed through no fault of your own, which qualifies you. Certify every two weeks. You're in this phase while you're still able and available to work — even if light duty.

State Disability Insurance

~4 weeks before due date → Transition to SDI

When your doctor certifies you cannot perform your regular job duties, stop certifying for UI and notify EDD you're no longer able and available for work. File your DE 2501. Do not file early without medical certification — denials are common. If complications arise earlier, your doctor can certify you sooner.

State Disability Insurance continues

Birth + 6–8 weeks → Recovery period

SDI continues through your postpartum recovery: typically 6 weeks after a vaginal delivery, 8 weeks after a C-section. If complications occur, your doctor can certify additional weeks.

Paid Family Leave

When doctor clears you → File for PFL immediately

Don't wait. Log into your SDI Online account around a week before SDI ends and look for the DE 2501FP link. File it before your SDI claim closes. If the link isn't there, call EDD right away — payment gaps in this transition are common but avoidable.

Optional: Return to Unemployment

After PFL ends → Re-open UI claim if job searching

Since you were laid off (not quit), you can return to UI after PFL ends. You'll need to certify that you have childcare arrangements and are able to accept suitable work. UI can continue up to the remainder of your 26-week entitlement.

⚠️ Common mistakes that cost women their benefits

🚫

Never collect UI and SDI/PFL at the same time

This is considered fraud and will result in an overpayment notice, penalties, and stress you don't need. When you start SDI, stop certifying for UI immediately and notify EDD you are no longer available for work.

⏰

Don't file SDI too early (or too late)

Filing before your doctor has certified you as disabled leads to denial. Being pregnant alone is not disability — your doctor must confirm you cannot perform your regular work. File no earlier than 9 days after disability begins, and no later than 49 days to avoid losing benefits.

📋

Watch the SDI → PFL transition closely

EDD's system should automatically prompt you to file for PFL, but this transition fails for many women. If payments stop after SDI ends and you haven't started PFL, contact EDD immediately — this is a known system issue.

💵

Severance may affect your UI — report it correctly

If you're receiving severance, you may not be able to collect UI simultaneously. Report severance honestly on your claim. EDD will determine eligibility. Failing to report income leads to overpayment penalties.

🏥

Sort out health insurance immediately

Your employer coverage likely ends at termination or end of severance. You have 60 days to elect COBRA. If your income drops, you may qualify for Medi-Cal — apply through Covered California at coveredca.com. Do not let coverage lapse during your pregnancy.

Frequently Asked

Your questions answered

Real questions from women who've navigated this exact situation.

Can I get SDI and PFL if I'm already unemployed when I give birth?
Yes. Your eligibility for SDI and PFL is based on your base period wages — typically the 5–18 months before your claim start date — not your current employment status. As long as you earned at least $300 during that period with SDI deductions taken from your pay (shown as CASDI on your paystub), you qualify. Being laid off does not eliminate these benefits.
Will my benefits be lower because I'm not actively working?
No — SDI and PFL calculate your benefit based on your highest-earning quarter during the base period (your earnings from before the layoff). Your benefit amount is set based on historical wages, not what you're earning now. Expect 70–90% of your average earnings from that period, up to the weekly maximum ($1,765 in 2026).
Should I file the SDI claim before or after my last day/severance ends?
You should file SDI when your doctor certifies you're unable to work due to pregnancy — not based on employment dates. If your severance continues after your last day, you may have a gap between severance ending and SDI starting. During that gap, UI may be appropriate if you're still able to work. The key rule: you cannot file SDI until your doctor has confirmed disability, and you cannot collect UI and SDI simultaneously.
What if my company closes or HR is unresponsive?
Get your final paystub and any layoff documentation in writing immediately — before the company fully shuts down. EDD can verify your wage history through their own records (employers report wages quarterly), but having documentation yourself speeds up the process. If you can't reach HR, file your claims anyway and let EDD work through it. List all employers from your base period on your application.
Can I collect UI again after PFL ends?
Yes — if you were laid off (not fired for cause or resigned), you can reopen your UI claim after PFL ends. You'll need to certify that you are able and available for work, and EDD may ask about childcare arrangements. The remaining weeks of your 26-week UI entitlement can continue at that point.
Does claiming these benefits affect my immigration status?
SDI and PFL contributions come from your paycheck and are your earned benefits. They are generally not considered "public charge" benefits for immigration purposes. However, immigration law is complex and individual situations vary. Consult an immigration attorney if you have specific concerns. EDD states that information on SDI/PFL claims is confidential.
My doctor said I can work limited hours — do I still qualify for SDI?
Potentially, yes. If your licensed health professional certifies that reduced hours or duties are required due to your pregnancy, you may qualify for partial SDI benefits. You don't need to be completely unable to work — you just need a certified medical reason you cannot perform your full regular duties. Contact EDD for specifics on partial disability claims.
